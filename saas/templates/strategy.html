Strategy Guide

Front-Side Higher Timeframe Untouched Levels

1 Strategy Overview

The bot identifies untouched support and resistance levels on higher timeframes, waits for price to approach these levels for the first time (front-side), then uses a lower timeframe candle pattern to confirm entry.

Why "front-side"? The first time price visits a level, institutional orders are most likely to be sitting there. After the first touch, the level weakens because those orders have been absorbed.

2 Zone Discovery

Zones are identified using the SNR Frequency Analysis from the LumiTrade API. The system scans for support and resistance levels that have been historically significant but haven't been revisited recently.

What makes a valid zone:

  • Historical significance — price has reacted at this level multiple times
  • Untouched (front-side) — price hasn't visited this level recently
  • Higher timeframe — weekly/monthly zones are stronger than hourly
  • Within ATR tolerance — price must be close enough to the level to matter

5 Entry Triggers

A signal fires when ALL of the following conditions are met:

  1. Zone identified — HTF S/R level found via SNR Frequency analysis
  2. Price approaches zone — within ATR tolerance (zone activates)
  3. Trigger candle confirms — actionable pattern on the trigger timeframe
  4. Bias score passes minimum — trend + candle score >= threshold
  5. Risk:Reward passes minimum — entry to target vs entry to stop

How units are calculated:

  1. Risk amount = account balance x risk % (or fixed dollar amount)
  2. Stop distance converted to pips (JPY pairs normalized: pip = 0.01)
  3. Pip cost per unit calculated based on pair type (XXX/USD, USD/XXX, cross)
  4. Units = risk amount / (stop pips x pip cost per unit)

8 Exit Rules

Forex Exits

  • Stop Loss: Fixed at zone boundary (ATR-adjusted). Placed as part of the order.
  • Take Profit: Next significant S/R level. Placed as part of the order.
  • Both SL and TP are set at order placement — no monitoring needed.

Options Exits (Auto-Monitored)
